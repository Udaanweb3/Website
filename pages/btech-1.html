Blockchain Technology



Introduction

By definition, a Blockchain is a shared, immutable ledger that facilitates the process of recording transactions and tracking assets in a network.

In simple terms, a Blockchain is a continuous and growing list of records or transactions, called blocks. Multiple blocks are linked together using cryptography. In general, cryptography is the study of secure communication techniques that allow only the sender and recipient to view the content of the message.

Now, each block contains transaction data or timestamp. This timestamp is the very proof of the transaction. Multiple blocks in this way, form chains. This iterative process confirms the “integrity of the previous block”, all the way to the initial block, which is known as the genesis block.

What does Blockchain offer us?

Decentralisation: The blockchain eliminates third party interference and the risk that comes with data being handled centrally, by storing data across its peer to peer network.

Cryptocurrency: Blockchain technology is used to record transactions of cryptocurrencies. Thus, blockchain helps in the verification and traceability of multistep transactions, it also provides a secure transaction and speedy transaction.

Financial Services & Banks: Blockchain has the potential to replace traditional banking systems like SWIFT, providing efficient and secure transactions and storing data into a decentralised ledger.

Games: Video games use blockchain technologies like cryptocurrencies and NFTs for monetization. Here, NFTs are in the form of the player character, virtual plot of land, etc. which can be traded using in-game currency. However, certain risks involved in Blockchain and NFT based games are still under consideration as stated by major gaming publishers including Ubisoft, Electronic Arts, etc..
