Types of Blockchain Networks:

There are at least four types of Blockchain Networks,

Public Blockchain: This is the most transparent of all types of Blockchain. In a Public Blockchain, anyone from anywhere with access to the internet can view the ledger of all transactions and holders. Unlike banks, here the transactions from peer to peer are visible but the encrypted keys are only known to the executioner. Anyone can become a validator by mining on the network via certain hardware and earning miner rewards in the form of cryptocurrencies. This process is done via POW, POS & POB mechanisms.

For example, Bitcoin Blockchain & Ethereum Blockchain

Private Blockchain: Private Blockchain unlike Public Blockchain is the least transparent of all. Here, Only the people who are given permission by the developer or administrator has access to the ledger. There are one or more entities that control the network and this leads to reliance on third parties to transact. One benefit of this type of blockchain is its transaction fee is very less compared to the public blockchain. A lot of illegal activities are performed via Private blockchains.

For Example, Ripple & Hyperledger

Hybrid Blockchain: This type of Blockchain is a conjugation of Public & Private Blockchain, they can be Centralised or Decentralised. The main motive for building this blockchain is to take the ideal Part of Public & Private Blockchains.

Example, Dragonchain

Sidechains: This is the Blockchain we see and use the most in day to day lives. It is a term to define a Blockchain Ledger running in parallel to a Primary Blockchain. Entries from the primary blockchain (where said entries typically represent digital assets) can be linked to and from the sidechain; this allows the sidechain to otherwise operate independently of the primary blockchain (e.g., by using an alternate means of record keeping, alternate consensus algorithm, etc.). .

For example, Polygon Network.

Blockchain Analysis

With the increasing popularity and usage of cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, Ethereum, and other coins, the analysis of Blockchains has become significantly important. The process of understanding and regulating crypto has become an issue for many reserve banks and exchanges. A public blockchain provides high-level transparency to users, where one can access the blockchain data, but cannot interpret the data. This can lead to illegal dark market trades of drugs, weapons and money laundering. This however is believed can take place mostly in cryptocurrencies which are private and untraceable. However, leading tech companies are working on specialised systems to provide blockchain tracking services, a step towards regulating cryptocurrency. The question is about the public accessibility of blockchain data and the personal privacy of the very same data. Some countries are developing their own Blockchain technology for building their digital currency that will be equivalent to the fiat of the respective countries. It is a key debate in cryptocurrency and ultimately in the blockchain.
